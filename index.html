Intro

Nigeria cusiine is a vibrant and diverse tapestry of flavours, colors and textures. With a rich cultural and an array of ingredients. Nigeria food is a true refelction of the country's multifaceted identity. In this blog, we will take you on a culinary journey through the heart of Nigeria, exploring some of its most iconic dishes, ingredients, and cooking techniques.

Some of Nigeria Dishes

  1. Rice:This one-post dish is a Nigerian favourite. It feature rice cooked in a flavourful tomato sauce woth blend of spices, vegetables and often chicken or beef
  2. Egusi Soup: Made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, and various porteins. Egusi soup is a hearty and nutritious Nigerian staple.
  3. Moi-Moi:This steamed bean pudding is a popular side dish made from black-eyed peas, peppers, onions and spices..
  4. Amala:is a traditional Nigerian meal eaten by the Yoruba ethnic group in the country's western states. It's manufactured using unripe plantain flour, yam flour, and/or cassava flour.Amala goes well with variety of soups like Ewedu, Egusi, Eforiro, Ogbonno
  5. Bole & Grilled Fish:A classic Nigerian street food said to have originated in Port Harcourt, the capital city of Rivers State, Nigeria. This city is known for a lot of great things but the most outstanding feature of this great oil city is its local dishes, served with different accompaniment of choice usually a specially prepared stew and any protein of one's choice (commonly fish or chicken).In the Western part of the country, this delicious chow is served with nicely fried groundnut
